Whitepaper outline

Structure of the FL-BSA methodology paper covering science, controls, and deployment.

1. Executive summary

  • Purpose: deterministic bias simulation for regulated lenders.
  • Scope: FL-BSA appliance; no outbound connectivity by default.
  • Audience: Compliance, Model Risk, Internal Audit, Supervisors.

2. Data pipeline & modelling

The pipeline ingests structured lending data or summary statistics, applies dual-branch CTGAN calibration, and records model artefacts with reproducible seeds. Sections document preprocessing, protected characteristic handling, disparate impact metrics, and reproducibility checkpoints.

3. Controls & assurance

  • Data boundary: runs in-customer VPC; no default telemetry.
  • Hash chain: dataset → calibration artefacts → generated outputs.
  • Evidence: PDF, signed manifest, certificate appendix.
  • Governance tie-ins: FCA, EU AI Act, ECOA references.

4. Deployment patterns

Outlines AWS Marketplace AMI and docker-compose options, including hardened baseline, IAM integration, and optional Prometheus endpoints. Includes change-control checklist and incident response escalation paths.

FAQ

Do we send any data to Equilens?

No. FL-BSA is self-hosted; all compute happens in your VPC/VM. Zero outbound calls by default.

How long does a typical run take?

From minutes for 10k rows to approximately 12 minutes for around 1M rows on validated hardware profiles; daily monitoring runs reuse the generator and complete in ≤5 minutes.

What regulatory frameworks are covered?

Evidence maps to ECOA/Reg B (US), EU AI Act, and FCA Consumer Duty. Certificates include Data Quality, Model Fidelity, Training Convergence, Synthetic Quality, and Regulatory Alignment.

What are the deployment options?

AWS Marketplace AMI or containerized via docker-compose. CPU-only and GPU-preferred profiles supported.

What’s in the evidence bundle?

A 30-page PDF report, a signed manifest (dataset hash, RNG seed, software version), and certificates. Artifacts are chained for auditability and can be retrieved by Task ID.
